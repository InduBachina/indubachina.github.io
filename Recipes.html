Chicken 65

Chef: Ravi Kumar

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • Yogurt
  • Spices (chili powder, turmeric, garam masala)
  • Corn flour
  • Curry leaves
  • Oil for frying

Recipe

Marinate bite-sized pieces of boneless chicken in a mixture of ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, lemon juice, yogurt, corn flour, cumin powder, and salt. Let the chicken marinate for at least 1 hour. Next, heat oil for deep frying in a pan. Once the oil is hot, fry the marinated chicken pieces until they are cooked through. Fry in batches to ensure even cooking. Once fried, drain excess oil. Optionally, sauté curry leaves and sliced green chilies in a separate pan, then toss the fried chicken pieces with the curry leaves and green chilies for added flavor. Serve hot as an appetizer.

TIPS
  • Marinate the chicken with yogurt and spices for at least 2 hours for best results.
  • Fry the chicken in batches to ensure even cooking.
  • Drain excess oil on paper towels before serving.

Egg Biryani

Chef: Priya Patel

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Eggs
  • Spices (cardamom, cinnamon, cloves)
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ghee

Recipe

Boil eggs until they're hard-boiled, then peel and set them aside. In a separate pan, sauté sliced onions, tomatoes, and spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder until fragrant. Add soaked basmati rice to the mixture along with water and salt, then let it cook until the rice is almost done. Next, layer the boiled eggs on top of the rice and sprinkle with chopped coriander leaves and fried onions. Cover and let it simmer until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors have melded together. Serve hot with raita or salad for a satisfying meal.

TIPS
  • Soak rice for at least 30 minutes before cooking for better texture.
  • Hard-boil eggs separately before adding to the biryani.
  • Garnish with fried onions and fresh cilantro before serving.

Fish Pulusu

Chef: Ananya Rao

Ingredients

  • Fish fillets
  • Tamarind
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Spices (coriander, cumin, turmeric)
  • Curry leaves

Recipe

Marinate fish with turmeric and salt. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until golden, then add tomatoes and spices like cumin, coriander, chili powder, and garam masala. Pour in coconut milk and water, simmer, then add fish and cook until tender. Garnish with coriander and serve hot with rice or naan. Adjust seasoning to taste.

TIPS
  • Use fresh fish for the best flavor.
  • Let the curry simmer on low heat for at least 20 minutes for flavors to meld.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice for a delicious meal.

Gulab Jamun

Chef: Aisha Khan

Ingredients

  • Milk powder
  • All-purpose flour
  • Sugar
  • Cardamom
  • Rose water
  • Saffron

Recipe

Combine khoya and flour to form a dough, shape into small balls, and fry until golden brown. In a separate pot, prepare a sugar syrup with water and sugar, flavored with rose water. Soak the fried balls in the syrup until they absorb it, then serve warm or at room temperature. Optionally garnish with nuts or rose petals before serving.

TIPS
  • Add a pinch of baking soda to the dough for softer gulab jamuns.
  • Soak the fried gulab jamuns in warm sugar syrup for at least 2 hours for best results.
  • Garnish with chopped nuts before serving.
